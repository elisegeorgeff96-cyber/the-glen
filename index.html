You gave years to work that asked everything of you — work that was genuinely important, and genuinely hard. You gave until there was nothing left, and then you gave some more.

Now you are standing at a tree line. Behind you: the work. In front of you: a forest you do not recognize. And in the dark between the branches, something speaks — not loudly, and not with answers. Just a single instruction:

Follow the voice to the glen. We will figure out the rest from there.

This compass is for the exhausted and brilliant. There are no wrong paths. Some options are practical, some are strange, and the ones marked ✦ wildcard are for when your imagination deserves room to run further than your caution allows.


▶The Glen — First Question
THE VOICE ASKS:
"WHAT BROUGHT YOU
TO THE TREE LINE?"
Before the compass can point anywhere, it needs to know where you're coming from. Not what you achieved — how it actually feels right now, standing in the dark.

★The Shadow Question
SOMETHING WAS
DONE TO YOU.
Not everything that drained you was the work itself. Sometimes the environment was impossible, but it was a specific person who broke something. The forest wants to know if you are carrying that too.

◈The Work Itself

Not what you're good at — you already know that. What kind of work leaves you feeling like yourself at the end of a day?

○Other Humans
HOW MUCH OF YOU DO
OTHER PEOPLE GET?
You have spent years managing relationships across organizations, cultures, and people who did not always make it easy. Now — genuinely — how much of your energy do you want to give to other people in the next chapter?

✳The Scholar's Question

You have a graduate degree — maybe more than one. You have spent years being the person who had done the reading. What calls you forward from here?

♦The Relief Question

✦The Glen's Gift

WHICH DO YOU TAKE?
You don't have to know what it's for. You don't have to explain your choice. Pick the one that your hand reaches for first — or the one that makes the least rational sense. The glen has been watching. It already knows something you don't.
